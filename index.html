Research

Can we build models of the cell that stay right outside the data they were trained on?

Virtual-cell efforts lean on perturbation biology as the first way to interrogate what's alive. But the design space — gene combinations, dosages, cell types, contexts — is combinatorial, and today's generative models degrade sharply off-distribution. No free lunch says you can't generalize from nothing.

So the prior has to come from biology itself. I want models constrained by signaling-pathway architecture, gene-regulatory logic, and evolutionary structure — inductive biases that hold a model's behavior in check in regimes it was never shown.

It's a problem that lives between three fields, and I'm trying to work in all three at once.

Selected work

Four projects, each a different way of pulling structure out of biological data.

AI in the Life Sciences · Elsevier2025First author

Predicting bacterial antagonism from biofilm shape A machine-learning framework for the prediction and analysis of bacterial antagonism in biofilms using morphological descriptors.

Can you tell whether a beneficial Bacillus strain will out-compete a pathogen from the morphology of single-species biofilms alone — without exhaustive co-culture screening? I built the framework that does it, designed a homology-controlled cross-validation scheme to stop the model cheating on closely related strains, and used SHAP and permutation importance to read out which morphological features actually drive exclusion. It got picked up by INRAE/Micalis as part of a dual imaging-and-AI breakthrough.

ExamineMyClusters — explaining patient subgroups Explainable clustering for clinical data.

Clustering tells a clinician that patients fall into subgroups; it rarely tells them why. EMC is a Python package that explains clusters by combining feature-importance methods (SHAP, permutation importance, PD/ALE), rule extraction and prototypes — with two methodological additions of my own: a SHAP-based contrast that highlights what separates two clusters, and an automatic way to pick the right importance method using a rank-stability metric. Built for small, rare-disease-scale cohorts in the MediTwin effort.

Owkin × Servier2025★ AI Methodology Award

Multimodal representation learning for glioblastoma Contrastive patient embeddings across histopathology, transcriptomics and genomics.

With four teammates at a research hackathon, a contrastive framework (InfoNCE / NT-Xent) that learns patient-level representations across H&E slides, bulk RNA-seq, whole-exome sequencing and spatial transcriptomics. The data is brutally low-N, high-P, so we leaned on foundation-model encoders (Bioptimus, scFoundation, Novae) and fought representation collapse with modality-wise dropout and variance–covariance regularization. The jury gave it the methodology award.

TELLAM — reading the repeats genomes usually discard An RNA-seq pipeline for retrotransposon expression in senescent cells.

A bioinformatics pipeline to quantify retrotransposon expression in senescent cells — the repetitive-element signal that standard RNA-seq workflows tend to throw away, but that may matter for how cells age.
